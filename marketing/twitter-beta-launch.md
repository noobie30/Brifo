## Day 3 — Wednesday · Auto-Detection (the "no bot" thread)

**Format:** Thread (5 tweets) — this is the shareable one of the week
**Attach:** 15-second screen recording on tweet 5 (reuse Instagram Day 3 reel)

```
(1/5) Why Brifo doesn't use a meeting bot.

Every AI notetaker today joins the call as a bot. Fireflies. Otter. Fathom. Granola (extension-based but same issue on some calls).

We went the other way. Here's why ↓
```

```
(2/5) When a bot joins the waiting room before any human, 3 things happen:

• People speak more carefully
• IT blocks the domain
• Guests feel surveilled

You lose the candour that makes meetings worth having.
```

```
(3/5) So Brifo runs on your Mac. Not in the meeting.

It watches for the signals that a call is active — across Zoom, Meet, Teams, Slack, Discord — and captures locally. Your guests see nothing. IT sees no new vendor. The call stays the call.
```

```
(4/5) The trade-off: we can't capture every speaker's label perfectly if the audio is muddy.

The win: zero social cost, zero compliance friction, zero vendor fatigue. For most teams, that's a better deal than perfect diarisation.
```

```
(5/5) Here's what it looks like end-to-end.
```

## Daily engagement rhythm (15 min/day, non-negotiable)

X / Twitter reach is 90% about engagement, not posting. Every day:

1. **Reply to 10 tweets** in your niche — Granola/Fathom/Fireflies mentions, "meeting notes" searches, PM/founder pain tweets, India SaaS founders. **No pitching.** Add one useful thought, then move on.
2. **Bookmark-and-quote** 1 Indian SaaS founder's post per day. Builds relationships with people who matter.
3. **Check @brifoapp mentions** every morning & evening, reply within 30 min
4. **Follow 20 Indian SaaS founders / PMs / engineering leads per day** for the first week — many follow back and see your content
