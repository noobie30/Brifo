## Day 2 — Tuesday · The Admin Tax

### LinkedIn

There is a cost to every meeting that nobody talks about.

It is not the time spent in the room. It is the time spent afterward, reconstructing what was said, translating decisions into tasks, opening Jira, writing the ticket, guessing at the priority, chasing the person who said they would own it.

If you run six meetings a day, that process eats 90 minutes minimum. Across a week, across a team, it adds up to the equivalent of a full headcount doing nothing but transcribing spoken decisions into written ones.

We built Brifo to eliminate that category entirely. It listens to the meeting, writes the summary, and queues every action item as a pre-filled Jira ticket. You review it once after the call and approve what looks right. That is the whole workflow.

Nobody should be typing up a standup at 6 PM.

## Day 3 — Wednesday · Feature Deep-Dive: Auto-Detection

### LinkedIn

Why Brifo does not use a meeting bot.

Most meeting AI runs inside the call. That is also where most of its problems start. When a bot joins before the first human does, people notice. They censor themselves. Conversations get careful. The candid stuff, the stuff that actually moves decisions forward, stops happening out loud. On top of that, IT teams often block the domain, and guests feel a low-level surveillance they cannot quite name.

Brifo takes a different approach. It runs on your Mac, not in the meeting. It watches for signals that a call is active, starts capturing locally, and your guests never know it is there. No waiting room bot. No new entry on anyone's blocked domain list. The meeting stays the same. Only the notes change.

When you hang up, everything is ready. That was the brief from the start: notes without intrusion.

## Day 4 — Thursday · Feature Deep-Dive: Jira Push

### LinkedIn

There is a small gap between "we agreed to do this" and "it is in the backlog." That gap is where most action items disappear.

The meeting ends at 10:15. By 10:20 you are in the next one. By 5 PM the four things you committed to have blurred together. By next Monday's standup, two of them are quietly missing and nobody quite remembers who was supposed to own them.

Brifo closes that gap automatically. Every action item that surfaces during the meeting becomes a draft Jira issue with the summary written, priority inferred, issue type selected, and description tied to the specific moment in the transcript where it was raised. After the call you open the queue, review each one, and approve what is accurate. One tap per task and it lands in your board.

No copy-paste. No tab-switching. No "I will file it after the next meeting" that never actually happens.

No more "who was going to own that?" at the next standup.

## Day 6 — Saturday · Behind the Build

### LinkedIn

Why we built Brifo.

Last year our team tracked where every hour went. The meetings were expected. What surprised us was the 12 hours a week of downstream work: typing up decisions, copying action items into Jira, chasing people for context they thought someone else had captured, rewriting notes that were only half there to begin with.

We tried every AI notetaker in the market. The core problem was the same across all of them: a bot sitting visibly in the call, changing how people talked. And none of them pushed anything meaningful into the tools we actually lived in.

So we built what we needed. A macOS app that stays out of the meeting entirely, writes notes locally on the device, and pushes what matters into Jira without a second pass.
