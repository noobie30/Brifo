Bot recording vs local capture — what changes

Fireflies.ai joins your meeting as "Fred." Participants see a new person in the call. In client meetings and external interviews, that can feel awkward or raise consent questions. Some customers ask Fred to leave.

Brifo runs on your Mac and listens to the audio already playing through your speakers. No one sees a bot. You are still responsible for informing participants and complying with recording laws in your jurisdiction — but Brifo doesn't force the bot into the conversation.

When to pick each

Pick Fireflies.ai if…

  • You're in sales, RevOps, or a sales-adjacent role.
  • You live in Salesforce or HubSpot.
  • You need conversation intelligence — talk ratios, keyword tracking, sentiment.
  • Your team is on mixed OSes (Windows, Mac, Linux).
  • You're fine with Fred joining every meeting.

Pick Brifo if…

  • You're on a Mac (Apple Silicon).
  • You don't want a bot in your meetings.
  • You live in Jira — action items should file themselves.
  • You want audio to stay local, not uploaded.
  • You want free during beta, with beta pricing locked in for early users.

FAQ

Can I use both Brifo and Fireflies?

Technically yes, but it's redundant — you'll end up with two transcripts of the same meeting. Pick one per workflow.

Does Brifo have Salesforce integration?

Not today. If this is a must-have for you, Fireflies is the better pick right now. If you only need Jira, Brifo has that out of the box.

Which has better transcription accuracy?

Comparable for most use cases in English. Fireflies has had longer to tune multi-speaker diarization. Brifo leans on OpenAI GPT-4.1 post-processing on locally-captured audio, which is high-quality out of the box.

What about security — which company is more mature?

Fireflies is a mature commercial product with SOC 2 Type II and HIPAA options. Brifo is a beta product and does not yet hold those compliance certifications. If you're buying for a regulated org, Fireflies is the safer pick today.
