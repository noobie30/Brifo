Bot vs bot-free — why it matters

Otter.ai joins your Zoom/Meet/Teams call as a visible participant named "Otter.ai". Everyone in the meeting sees the bot, and some clients or customers find this awkward or raise consent questions. In regulated industries (finance, healthcare, legal) the bot can be a dealbreaker.

Brifo takes a different approach: it runs on your Mac and captures the system audio that's already playing on your speakers. Your meeting sees nothing unusual — no new participant, no "recording" banner, no consent pop-up at join time. You are still responsible for telling participants you're taking notes (and complying with local laws), but Brifo doesn't force the bot into the room.

When to pick each

Pick Otter.ai if…

  • Your team is on Windows or Linux (Brifo is Mac-only).
  • You want mobile access to transcripts on the go.
  • You want a mature product with years of polish.
  • You're fine with a bot in every meeting.
  • You need multi-speaker diarization with tuned accuracy.

Pick Brifo if…

  • You work on a Mac (Apple Silicon) and stay on Mac.
  • You don't want a bot in your meetings — for client-comfort, privacy, or policy reasons.
  • You want meetings captured automatically without pre-scheduling.
  • You use Jira and want action items filed in one click.
  • You want audio to stay local and not leave your device.
  • You want it free right now (during beta).

Frequently asked questions

Is Brifo a replacement for Otter.ai?

If you're on Mac and don't need web/mobile access, yes. Brifo covers the core of what Otter does — transcription, AI summaries, action items — and adds auto-detection, local audio, and Jira integration. If you rely on Otter's mobile app or cross-OS team features, Brifo isn't a drop-in replacement.

Can I import my Otter transcripts into Brifo?

Not today. If you want this, email hello@brifo.in — we're prioritizing features users ask for.

Does Brifo work with Zoom the same way Otter does?

Brifo works with Zoom without being invited to the call. Open Zoom, start your meeting, Brifo auto-detects and captures — no bot joins. Otter typically joins Zoom via calendar invite or the Otter Zoom app.

How accurate is Brifo's transcription?

Brifo uses a locally-captured high-quality audio stream piped through an ASR + GPT-4.1 post-processing pipeline. Accuracy is comparable to Otter's for English on a typical Mac microphone. Both improve with cleaner audio.

Which tool is more privacy-preserving?

Brifo, by design. Brifo processes audio locally and only sends the transcript to its cloud; raw audio never leaves your Mac. Otter uploads full audio to its cloud. If your organization has a strict "no cloud audio" policy, Brifo is the easier pick.
